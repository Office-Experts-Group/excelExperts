Executive Summary

This report presents findings from a full audit of excelexperts.com.au, combining a live page-by-page content review with three months of Google Search Console data. The objective was to identify why programmer work enquiries are lower than expected, and what actions would most effectively address this.

Key finding: SEO is not the primary problem. The site ranks well for its target audience — the homepage ranks first nationally for 'excel consultant near me', location pages fill subsequent positions, and the site dominates AI Overview results with direct citations. The problem lies in what happens after the click.

The GSC data reveals 88,608 impressions over three months and 679 total clicks. However, 538 of those clicks (79%) went to blog posts — primarily a single article about Excel on Mac — while all seven combined service pages received just 15 clicks. This is a significant imbalance. The service pages that drive programmer enquiries are being found, but are not compelling enough to earn clicks at scale, and once visited, some lack the content depth to convert a prospective client.

The content audit found a clear quality split. Three service pages — VBA & Macro Development, Upgrades & Migration, and Excel Support & Training — are well-written, technically specific, and would stand comparison with any Australian competitor. These pages have been maintained and improved in recent months. The remaining four service pages, particularly Data Manipulation and Pivot Tables & Reporting, contain very thin content that does not adequately describe the work the team is capable of, and are losing potential programming enquiries as a result.

The recommended approach is therefore not to chase more traffic, but to improve what happens to the traffic already arriving — by strengthening weak service pages, creating targeted content for high-impression zero-click queries, and building content that converts research-intent visitors into project enquiries.

Service Page Content Audit

Each of the seven service pages was reviewed in full by fetching and analysing the live page content. Pages are assessed against three criteria: content depth, keyword alignment with programmer-intent queries, and conversion readiness.

Overall Finding

There is a clear quality split on the site. Three pages have been actively maintained and improved and are performing well. Four pages remain in their original state and are materially underperforming. This distinction is important: the problem is not a site-wide failure but a specific content debt on a subset of pages.

Pages in Strong Condition

The following three pages are well-written, technically specific, and would stand comparison with any Australian competitor in this space. They address programmer-level buyers directly, describe specific capabilities, and include relevant use cases.

VBA & Macro Development — Strong

The best page on the site. Coverage of form creation, toolbar customisation, external data connections, web-based data retrieval, user validation, automated chart and report generation, and data sorting algorithms is thorough and technically credible. The language is specific enough that a buyer who knows what they need will immediately recognise this team can do it.

Minor gaps: no mention of Power Query or Office Scripts (Microsoft's modern alternatives to VBA for Microsoft 365 environments); meta description does not include Australia; add-in content overlaps with the dedicated add-in page.

Upgrades & Migration — Strong

Good practical depth. The specific mention of 32/64-bit compatibility issues, ActiveX control resizing, macro failures after version changes, and workbook performance degradation maps directly to problems clients search for when they are in pain. The 'Migrating Away From Excel' section is a smart strategic addition that positions the team across the full migration journey and creates natural cross-links to Power Platform and Access.

Opportunity: the Power Platform and Access cross-sell is buried at the bottom. Moving this higher, and updating the page title to reference Microsoft 365 migration, would improve both the conversion path and search visibility.

Excel Support & Training — Strong

Solid scope covering on-demand technical assistance, code reviews, documentation, migration support, and data conversion. The training section differentiates well with the mention of custom 'How To' videos. The acknowledgement that clients can get help at any level — individual, team, or department — broadens the appeal effectively.

Gap: Power Query support is not mentioned anywhere on this page, despite being one of the most common help requests from Excel-using businesses. Training topics and levels are not described specifically enough for a prospective client to self-qualify.

Pages Requiring Attention

The following four pages have not been updated since the site was originally built and are losing enquiries as a result. They are not failing from an SEO perspective — several have reasonable impression counts — but they are failing to convert visitors once they arrive.

Formulas & Custom Formulas — Needs Updating

The page has a good foundation. The explanation of why formula-heavy workbooks cause problems, and the 'why use custom formulas' rationale, is well written. The FAQs are relevant and useful. However, the page has two significant gaps that date it.

First, there is a Vimeo video embed from 2018 that is likely to show outdated Excel UI and may not play correctly in modern browsers. Second, the page makes no mention of LAMBDA, LET, XLOOKUP, or dynamic array functions — the modern Excel 365 formula capabilities that are now what advanced users search for help with. A buyer researching LAMBDA-based solutions will not see this page as current.

Add-in Development — Needs Updating

The page has good structural bones, explaining the difference between VBA add-ins and VSTO add-ins clearly, with a relevant FAQ. However, it makes no mention of Office JS (the JavaScript API add-in architecture that Microsoft now promotes as the modern standard for Excel add-ins, replacing VSTO for web-compatible solutions).

Any enterprise client or ISV asking about add-in development in 2025–26 may specifically be asking about Office JS. The absence of this term from the page creates a credibility gap with sophisticated buyers, even if the team is fully capable of delivering Office JS work. The page title is also weak for search: 'Add-in Development - Microsoft Excel' does not target the specific queries shown in the GSC data (custom excel add-ins australia, excel add-in developer australia).

Pivot Tables, Charts & Reporting — Needs Rewrite

This page is materially thin. The main body consists of two short paragraphs and a bullet list of tools: Excel Charts, Pivot Tables, Power Query, PowerPivot, Power BI. There is no description of what a reporting engagement actually involves, no explanation of what Power Query work looks like from a client's perspective, and no differentiation between a quick pivot table fix and a full automated reporting solution build.

This is a significant problem because the services listed — particularly custom dashboard development and Power Query automation — are high-value programmer work. A client searching for dashboard development who lands on this page will not find enough information to understand what they would get, and will likely leave. Power Query alone warrants a dedicated page.

Data Manipulation — Requires Complete Rewrite

This is the weakest page on the site by a substantial margin. The entire body of the page is one paragraph and a short bullet list. There is no explanation of what a data manipulation engagement involves, no use cases, no technology depth, and no description of outcomes. The word 'Power Query' does not appear anywhere on the page, despite Power Query being the primary modern Excel tool for data transformation and one of the most searched Excel-related service queries in Australia.

The GSC data confirms this: the page has only 73 impressions and zero clicks over three months. It has almost no search presence. The VBA & Macro Development page should be used as the quality benchmark for a rewrite — same structure, same specificity, applied to data transformation work.

Cross-Cutting Issues

The following issues were identified across multiple pages and affect the site as a whole.

Power Query is almost invisible — Power Query appears as a single bullet point on the Pivot Tables page and one brief mention on the Upgrades page. It has no dedicated page, no depth on any service page, and is not associated with the programming skillset anywhere. Power Query is the most searched Excel automation topic in Australia and the site is effectively absent for these queries. This is the largest single content gap.

Office Scripts not mentioned anywhere — Office Scripts is Microsoft's modern alternative to VBA for Excel on the web and Microsoft 365. It does not appear on any page. Enterprise clients and IT managers evaluating M365 automation options may specifically be looking for Office Scripts capability.

No case studies or project examples — The testimonials are strong but generic. No page shows a before-and-after, a project scope, or a specific measurable outcome. For programmer-intent buyers — someone with a specific build requirement — concrete examples are the primary trust signal that converts a visitor to an enquiry. The Custom Design & Development page is the natural home for this content.

Custom Design & Development page is a service list, not a destination — This page lists the same services as the individual pages without adding value. Several of its quick links point off-site to officeexperts.com.au. It ranks for nothing specific and converts at a very low rate. Repurposing it as a project showcase would give it a clear reason to exist and a conversion function.

Upgrades page title misses a key term — 'Excel Upgrades and Migration' does not reference Microsoft 365. As organisations move to M365, 'Microsoft 365 Excel migration' is an increasingly searched query. Updating the title and adding a paragraph specifically addressing M365 migration scenarios would capture this growing query cluster.

Recommendations

Recommendations are separated into two categories: conversion improvements (what happens after the click) and content/SEO improvements (what gets clicked in the first place). Conversion is addressed first because it delivers faster results from existing traffic.

A. Conversion Improvements

These changes address the gap between visits and enquiries. They do not require new pages or significant SEO work — they improve what happens on pages people are already visiting.

Rewrite the Data Manipulation page — The page is currently so thin it actively undermines credibility. A rewrite using the VBA page as a quality template — covering Power Query, ETL workflows, SQL integration, API data pulls, bulk conversions — would give this page a reason to convert visitors. Estimated effort: one focused session. This is the highest-effort-to-impact ratio change available.

Expand the Pivot Tables & Reporting page significantly — Double the content. Describe what a dashboard build engagement looks like, what Power Query work involves, what the client receives at the end. Consider whether this should be split into two pages: one for pivot/reporting work and one for dashboard development specifically. Both represent distinct programmer services.

Update the Add-in page with an Office JS section — Add a third add-in type alongside VBA and VSTO covering Office JS/JavaScript API add-ins. One to two paragraphs and an additional FAQ entry. This closes a credibility gap with enterprise buyers and is a low-effort change to an otherwise solid page.

Add modern formula functions to the Formulas page — Add a section covering LAMBDA, LET, XLOOKUP, and dynamic arrays. These are what advanced Excel 365 users are currently searching for help with. Also review the 2018 Vimeo embed and replace or remove it if the UI is outdated.

Convert Custom Design & Development into a project showcase — Replace the service list format with three to four anonymised project case studies. Include the problem presented, what was built, and the measurable outcome (hours saved, errors eliminated, processes automated). This is the highest-converting content type for programmer-level work and is entirely absent from the site currently.

B. Content & SEO Improvements

These changes address the query gaps identified in the GSC data and add new pages to capture searches the site is currently missing.

Create a dedicated Power Query consulting page — Target: /power-query-consulting. This is the single largest keyword gap on the site. The page should cover what Power Query does, when it is the right solution versus VBA, types of engagements (ETL pipelines, data cleaning, report automation, multi-source consolidation), and a clear call to action. This page captures a different buyer to the VBA page — more analyst-level, larger organisations, often larger projects.

Create a dedicated Excel dashboard development page — Target: /excel-dashboard-development. Clients searching specifically for dashboard development have a build project in mind — this is programmer work. Currently this service is buried inside the Pivot Tables page behind pivot table help copy. A dedicated page signals specialisation and captures project-intent queries directly.

Address the zero-click high-impression problem on existing pages — The GSC data shows several queries where the site appears at position 1–5 but earns zero clicks — most notably 'custom excel add-ins australia' (position 1.4, zero clicks) and 'excel consultant australia' (position 1.0, zero clicks). This is consistent with AI Overview absorption. The recommended response is to ensure page content is structured to be cited within AI Overviews — clear H2 question-format headings, direct concise answers, and factual specificity — rather than chasing ranking positions.

Redirect blog traffic to services — The Mac-related blog post is generating 501 clicks from an audience that is overwhelmingly not the target market. A strategically placed internal link from this post to relevant service pages (with an offer of remote support for Mac-specific Excel issues) would convert a small percentage of this high-volume traffic into commercial enquiries at zero additional cost.

Conclusion

The site is in a stronger position than the headline numbers might suggest. Strong local search rankings, AI Overview presence, and a set of well-maintained service pages provide a solid foundation. The opportunity is not to rebuild from scratch, but to address a specific and well-defined content debt on a small number of pages that represent the highest-value services.

The core recommendation is to treat the three underperforming service pages — Data Manipulation, Pivot Tables & Reporting, and to a lesser extent Formulas — as priority rewrites, and to add two new pages (Power Query consulting and Excel dashboard development) that the existing traffic and ranking infrastructure will support immediately on publication.

These are content and conversion improvements, not SEO campaigns. The traffic is already arriving. The work required is to ensure that when a prospective programming client lands on a service page, they find enough specific, credible, technically accurate content to pick up the phone or submit an enquiry.

Estimated timeline: the rewrite and expansion work for existing pages (items 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9) can be completed within 2–3 weeks. The two new pages (items 2 and 7) would require an additional 1–2 weeks of content and development work. The case study page (item 8) depends on gathering and anonymising client project details.
